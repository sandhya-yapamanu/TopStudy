About Indian Institute of Science

  1. After an accidental meeting between Jamsetji Tata and Swami Vivekananda, on a ship in 1893 where they discussed Tata's plan of bringing the steel industry to India
  2. Tata wrote to Vivekananda five years later: "I trust, you remember me as a fellow-traveller on your voyage from Japan to Chicago.
  3. The land and other facilities for the institution were donated on behalf of the State of Mysore by Krishna Raja Wadiyar IV, and Tata himself.
  4. The State of Mysore donated about 371 acres (150 ha)[15] of land. Tata gave several buildings towards the creation of IISc.
  5. The 7th Nizam of Hyderabad- Mir Osman Ali Khan also contributed in terms of money which amounted to 3 lakh Rupees over a period of 31 years
  6. The IISc campus is located in the north of Bangalore, about 6 kilometers from Bangalore City Railway Station and Kempegowda Bus Station, on the way to Yeshwantpur.
  7. The institute is about 35 kilometers from Kempegowda International Airport
  8. A number of other research institutes, Raman Research Institute, Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), Wood Research Institute and Central Power Research Institute (CPRI), are close to IISc
  9. The campus features six canteens (cafeterias), a gymkhana (gymnasium and sports complex), a football ground and a cricket ground, four dining messes (halls), one multi cuisine restaurant, nine men's and five women's hostels , an air strip
  10. The building, as one of the prominent landmarks of Bangalore, was designed by C. F. Stevens and Company of Bombay in 1912–13
  11. The buildings for the metallurgy and aerospace departments were designed by the German architect Otto Königsberger in 1940

Why it is Popular?

It is one of the oldest and finest centres of its kind in India, and has a high international standing in the academic world as well. IISc is renowned for its academic freedom, focus on research excellence, and the high quality of its faculty and students. At a broad level, it operates in a manner similar to that of premier international research schools, with both faculty hiring and promotions based on rigorous assessments from leading international experts in the area. Similarly, student selection is based on highly-competitive examinations (such as GATE or CSIR or UGC) and/or detailed technical interviews.
